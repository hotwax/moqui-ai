# The Composer Assistant

*A tech-agnostic overview for business and solution teams.*

---

## In one sentence

The Composer Assistant lets the people who know your business **create their own AI assistants — just by describing what they want** — and those assistants actually work inside your operations, safely, from minute one.

## The problem it solves

Every team has a backlog of *"I wish the system could just…"* — flag the orders about to miss their ship date, draft replies to "where's my order?", catch returns that don't match the original sale, summarize what went wrong overnight. Each one is obvious to the person who runs that area.

But today, making the software do any of it means a project: explain it to IT, wait in a development queue, test, release. **The people who understand the work are the furthest from being able to automate it.**

## What it is

A conversational co-pilot for building assistants. You tell it, in plain language, what you want an assistant to do for your business. It already understands your world — your orders, your returns, your locations, the words your company uses — so it asks a few sharp questions, proposes how the assistant should work, and hands you a **working assistant you can try on the spot.** No coding. No ticket. No waiting.

## The value it adds

- **Minutes, not months.** An idea becomes a working assistant in a single sitting — no development cycle.
- **It already knows your business.** Assistants are grounded in your real data and speak your company's language, so they're useful immediately — not generic.
- **It actually does the work.** These aren't chatbots that only talk; they take real actions in your operations, with the risky ones held for a human's approval.
- **Owned by the experts.** The people closest to the work build and refine their own assistants — no bottleneck, no translation loss.
- **Gets better over time.** It learns your company's vocabulary and the patterns your teams prefer, so each assistant you build is sharper than the last.

## How it works — four easy steps

1. **Describe it.** *"I want an assistant that flags pre-orders at risk of missing their promised date."*
2. **Answer a few questions.** It confirms what you mean and proposes the capabilities the assistant needs — in your terms.
3. **Try it live.** Run it right there on your real data and watch what it does. Refine in plain language until it's right.
4. **Put it to work.** Hand it to your team. Sensitive actions wait for human approval; everything it does is tracked.

## What people build with it

- **Fulfillment** — "Find orders stuck in the warehouse more than 24 hours and tell me why."
- **Customer care** — "Draft a friendly reply to every 'where is my order' message, with the real status."
- **Merchandising** — "Each morning, summarize yesterday's exceptions: cancellations, backorders, failed shipments."
- **Returns** — "Flag returns that don't match the original order and route them for review."
- **Pre-orders** — "Watch pre-orders and warn me when stock won't arrive in time to keep the promise."

## Why it's a step-change, not a small upgrade

- **vs. waiting on development** — no code, no queue; the expert builds it directly.
- **vs. generic AI tools** — those don't know your data and can't act on it; these are grounded in your real operations and do real work.
- **vs. rule-based automation** — no brittle if-this-then-that trees; you describe the goal, and it improves as your business teaches it.

## For consultants and solution teams

This is a demo and delivery superpower. In a sales conversation, you can build a prospect's own assistant **live** — *"let me show you one for your business"* — and watch the room change. In delivery, your domain expertise becomes working software the same day, not a change request. It shortens projects, deepens accounts, and makes your expertise the product.
